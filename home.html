About Me

When I got out of High School I went straight to the Art Institute of Pittsburgh to study graphic design. I found that graphic design wasn't exactly my thing, but I had gained a better understanding of modern digital design, and techniques. This helped me a lot as I have progressed down the road of a web and mobile application developer.

After a year and some months of working in the carpentry field I realized I needed to go back to school. When I went back to school the only degree that made sense to me was Information and Technology. I set out to learn networking and servers, but ended up falling in love with programming, and the logic conundrums it can present. Since starting at South Hills I have taken 7 different programming language classes that include: PHP, HTML5, CSS3, SQL, Java, Python, and C#. I continue to find new languages to play with and get basic understandings for.

Currently I have started working at a local software development company, ZedX, that designs digital solutions to agricultural problems around the world. I have been designing and implementing numerous internal applications that help the company function on a day to day basis. It feels great to see my work helping people every day. In the future I hope to help many more people.

I am all about the open source world, and try to help contribute to it all of the time. Digital solutions should be free to everyone, but if you like the work it doesn't hurt to support them :P. You can normally find me helping others on GitHub, jsFiddle, and many other open source development platforms.
